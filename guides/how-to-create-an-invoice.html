How to Create an Invoice: A Step-by-Step Guide

By Abdullah · June 2026 · 6 min read

An invoice is a formal request for payment that you send to a client after delivering a product or service. A clear, accurate invoice helps you look professional and, just as importantly, helps you get paid on time. This guide walks you through creating one from scratch.

Step 1: Add your business details

Start with who is sending the invoice. Include your business or personal name, address, email, phone number and any tax registration number (such as a GSTIN, VAT number or sales tax ID) that applies to you. If you have a logo, add it — it instantly makes the document look more trustworthy.

Step 2: Add your client's details

Under a "Bill To" heading, add the client's name, company, address and contact email. Sending the invoice to the right person (often someone in accounts payable) can dramatically speed up payment.

Step 3: Give the invoice a unique number

Every invoice should have a unique identifier, for example INV-2026-001. Sequential numbering keeps your records organised and makes it easy to reference a specific invoice later. Never reuse a number.

Step 4: Add issue and due dates

The issue date is when you create the invoice. The due date tells the client when payment is expected — for example, 15 or 30 days later. Clearly stated dates remove ambiguity and set expectations.

Step 5: List your products or services

For each item, include a short description, the quantity and the unit price. The amount for each line is simply quantity multiplied by price. Be specific in your descriptions; "Website homepage design" is clearer than "Design work".

Step 6: Apply tax and discounts

If you charge tax, add the correct percentage and make sure the calculation is shown separately. If you are offering a discount, decide whether it is a percentage or a flat amount and show it as a separate line so the client can see the breakdown.

Step 7: Show the total clearly

Summarise the subtotal, any discount, the tax and the final total. The grand total should be the most prominent figure on the invoice — it is the number the client cares about most.

Step 8: Add payment terms and notes

Use the notes area to state how you would like to be paid (bank transfer, UPI, etc.), your payment terms, and a short thank-you. A polite note such as "Payment due within 15 days. Thank you for your business!" is both professional and effective.

Step 9: Review, save and send

Double-check every figure, then export your invoice as a PDF and email it to your client. Keep a copy for your own records.
