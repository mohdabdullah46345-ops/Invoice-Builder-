Invoice vs Receipt: What's the Difference?

By Abdullah · June 2026 · 4 min read

People often use the words "invoice" and "receipt" as if they mean the same thing, but they serve very different purposes in a transaction. Understanding the difference helps you keep clean records and communicate clearly with clients.

What is an invoice?

An invoice is a request for payment. You send it before the client has paid, to tell them what they owe, for what, and by when. It typically includes an invoice number, itemised charges, taxes, the total due and the payment deadline.

What is a receipt?

A receipt is proof of payment. You issue it after the client has paid, confirming that the money was received. It usually shows the amount paid, the date of payment and the method used.

The key difference

In short: an invoice asks for money, while a receipt confirms money was received. The invoice comes first; the receipt comes last.

Side-by-side comparison

  • Purpose: Invoice = request payment · Receipt = confirm payment
  • Timing: Invoice = before payment · Receipt = after payment
  • Contains a due date: Invoice = yes · Receipt = no
  • Shows balance owed: Invoice = yes · Receipt = no (it shows amount paid)

Do you need both?

For most service businesses, yes. You send an invoice to request payment, and once the client pays, issuing a receipt is good practice — it gives them confirmation and gives you a clean paper trail for your accounting.
