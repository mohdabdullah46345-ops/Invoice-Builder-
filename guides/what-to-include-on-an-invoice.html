What to Include on an Invoice

By Abdullah · June 2026 · 5 min read

A professional invoice does more than ask for money — it gives your client every detail they need to approve and process the payment without coming back with questions. Missing information is one of the most common reasons invoices get delayed. Use this checklist to make sure yours is complete.

1. The word "Invoice"

It sounds obvious, but clearly labelling the document as an "Invoice" distinguishes it from a quote, estimate or receipt and helps the client route it correctly.

2. A unique invoice number

This is essential for your bookkeeping and for the client's records. Use a consistent format such as INV-2026-014.

3. Your business information

Include your name or company name, address, email, phone number and tax ID if applicable. This identifies who should be paid.

4. Your client's information

Add the client's name, company and address. Where possible, address it to a specific contact so it reaches the right desk quickly.

5. Issue date and due date

State when the invoice was created and when payment is due. Specific dates prevent the "I didn't realise it was overdue" excuse.

6. An itemised list of goods or services

Break the work down into clear line items, each with a description, quantity, unit price and line total. Transparency builds trust and reduces disputes.

7. Subtotal, tax and discounts

Show the subtotal before tax, any discount applied, the tax amount and rate, and then the final total. Keeping these separate makes the invoice easy to verify.

8. The total amount due

Make the grand total stand out. This is the single most important number for the person paying.

9. Payment terms and methods

Explain how you want to be paid (bank transfer, UPI, card, etc.) and your terms, such as "Net 15". Include your account details if relevant.

10. Notes or a thank-you

A short, friendly note leaves a good impression and can gently reinforce your payment terms.

Quick checklist

  • ✔ "Invoice" label and unique number
  • ✔ Your details and your client's details
  • ✔ Issue date and due date
  • ✔ Itemised services with quantities and prices
  • ✔ Subtotal, discount, tax and total
  • ✔ Payment method and terms
